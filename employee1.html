Karanja Wa Muiruri is a British-educated and legally qualified lawyer with extensive experience in corporate and commercial law. He obtained his LL.B from the University of the West of England (Bristol) and later pursued an LL.M in International Corporate Governance and Financial Regulation at Warwick University, where he graduated with First Class Honours (Distinction).

After moving back to Kenya in 2011, Karanja completed the necessary qualifications and was admitted to the Kenyan Bar in 2012, marking the start of his 12+ years of post-admission legal experience. He practiced in the corporate and commercial departments of some of Kenya’s most prestigious law firms, where he honed his expertise in mergers & acquisitions, corporate structuring, financial regulations, compliance, commercial transactions, and employment & labour law.

In September 2015, Karanja co-founded Karanja & Wanjiru Advocates with his then-partner, Irene Wanjiru Muiruri. Since then, he has led the firm as its Managing Partner and Head of Corporate & Commercial Law, advising clients on business setups, corporate restructuring, regulatory compliance, financial transactions, and employment law matters. He also provides ongoing legal counsel to both local and international clients, ensuring they remain compliant with Kenyan and international laws in their business operations.

Over the years, Karanja has worked closely with investors entering the Kenyan market, local and international businesses, and financial institutions on matters such as company formation, contract drafting, security perfection, regulatory compliance, employment contracts, workplace policies, and dispute resolution in employment matters.
